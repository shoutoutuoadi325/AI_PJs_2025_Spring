Color theory is a foundational concept in art and design that explores how colors interact, mix, and influence perception. It is based on principles like the color wheel, which organizes colors into primary (red, blue, yellow), secondary (mixtures like green, orange, purple), and tertiary hues. At its core, it examines relationships such as complementary (opposite colors for contrast), analogous (similar colors for harmony), and triadic (evenly spaced colors for balance) schemes.

In art, color theory plays a crucial role in:
- **Enhancing Composition**: Artists use it to create visual interest and emotional depth. For example, Vincent van Gogh employed complementary colors in "Starry Night" to make the scene vibrant and dynamic.
- **Evoking Emotions**: Colors carry psychological associations—warm tones like red and yellow can convey energy or warmth, while cool tones like blue and green suggest calm or serenity.
- **Guiding Decisions**: It helps in mixing paints, selecting palettes, and achieving desired effects, making artwork more intentional and impactful.

Beyond art, color theory is essential in fields like graphic design, fashion, and marketing. Designers apply it to influence consumer behavior—for instance, brands use red for urgency in sales promotions.
